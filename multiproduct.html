Things to keep in mind before buying a gold coin

Buying gold can be tricky unless you are buying them from Tanishq which guarantees the purity of gold. If you’re going to invest in a gold coin for yourself or looking to gift someone you care about, the below points might help you.

Purity of a gold coin

There are two ways to measure the purity of gold coins - Karat and fineness. Karat is the more commonly used measure to check the purity of gold. 24 Karat is considered to be the purest form of gold which contains 24/24 parts of gold. Similarly, 22KT gold contains 22 parts of gold and 2 parts of other metals such as zinc or silver to make it more durable while making gold jewellery. Whereas, Fineness is another parameter used to measure the purity of 24 Karat gold.

Hallmarking

Apart from purity, one should also check if the gold coins are hallmarked.

The government of India has set up the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) to ensure that consumers are not cheated while buying gold. BIS certifies gold coins and gold jewellery by stamping them to certify the level of purity of the item.

Looking to invest in a gold coin? Keep an eye on these guidelines.

a) BIS logo

b) Purity in carat and Fineness

c) Logo of Assaying and hallmarking centre

d) Jewellers' identification mark and number
